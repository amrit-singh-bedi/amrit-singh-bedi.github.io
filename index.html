I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science, with a joint appointment in Electrical and Computer Engineering, at the University of Central Florida. My research lies at the intersection of trustworthy AI, machine learning, optimization, and decision-making, with a particular emphasis on AI alignment, reinforcement learning, and safe generative AI.

My group develops mathematically grounded methods for building AI systems that are robust, adaptive, and deployable in real-world settings. We work on problems spanning alignment with human feedback, reliable inference-time control, multi-agent learning, and trustworthy embodied AI.

Before joining UCF, I was an Assistant Research Professor/Scientist at the University of Maryland, College Park. Earlier, I worked with the U.S. Army Research Laboratory. I received my Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur, where my thesis focused on distributed and online learning with stochastic gradient methods.

Research

My research focuses on machine learning and artificial intelligence, with an emphasis on trustworthy, adaptive, and efficient methods. I am particularly interested in advancing theoretical foundations and practical algorithms for reliable AI systems.

AI Alignment

Theoretical insights and practical algorithms for aligning AI systems with human goals and preferences.

RLHF

Reinforcement learning from human feedback, including robust and fair preference-based learning.

Safe Generative AI

Safety, trustworthiness, and inference-time safeguards for large language and multimodal models.

Optimization and Efficiency

Optimization methods and data-efficient machine learning for scalable and adaptive AI systems.
